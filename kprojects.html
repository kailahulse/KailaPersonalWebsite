ELVIS

Collaborating with a 15-member research team at Western Washington University to construct one of the largest virtual Internet simulations using Rust, despite having no prior experience in this area. The primary goal was to create a comprehensive model of the Internet, enabling simulations of DDOS attacks and the dark web to enhance online privacy. Our group has successfully tested and implemented smaller components of this ambitious research, including ARP routers, DNS, DHCP, TCP, and various network configurations. The overarching objective was to develop a highly complex and realistic Internet simulation. I have personally contributed to the research by playing a key role in building TcpStream and TcpListener components. Additionally, I utilized a Weibull Distribution formula to simulate user behavior while browsing the Internet. These components were rigorously tested in conjunction with a custom-built server, enabling seamless web browsing with HTTP links and embedded images. My new endeavor is to build a complex simulation that runs all the applications at the same time thus out research will reach new heights and be able to measure our speed and effectiveness of the applications and protocols the team has built.
